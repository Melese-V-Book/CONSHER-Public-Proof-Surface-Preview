# Proof-Surface Overview

In this bounded context, a public proof surface is a sanitized review-readable surface that shows how a bundle behaves under limits.

It is demo-only.

It is not proof of operational success.

It is not domain authority.

It is not operational proof.

It is not heritage conservation proof.

It is not field validation.

It is not stakeholder endorsement.

It is not production substrate.

The proof surface shows:

- what was admitted
- what was refused
- what was narrowed
- what was halted
- what remained non-binding
- what could not become consequence-bearing

#CONSHER is used as the example because the route demonstrates that a heritage-facing source can be made reviewable without becoming advisory, promotional, operational, interpretive, or authority-bearing.

The limits are part of the proof. A proof surface that loses its limits is no longer the same proof surface.

The author of #CONSHER is not a heritage conservation expert and does not claim heritage, ecclesiastical, public-safety, engineering, UNESCO, public, regulatory, archaeological, conservation, or operational authority.

The proof surface exists to show bounded route behavior, not to replace expert review, stakeholder dialogue, community input, institutional review, or domain-specific correction.
